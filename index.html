How Stablecoins
stay stable

A stablecoin is a cryptocurrency designed to maintain a fixed value — usually $1.00. But there are three radically different ways to achieve that peg, each with its own risks.

⬤ 3 mechanisms ⬤ Interactive ⬤ ~5 min read
01
The core
challenge
Crypto prices are volatile by nature — Bitcoin can swing 10% in a day. Yet many use cases (payments, lending, savings) require price certainty. Stablecoins solve this by engineering mechanisms that continuously correct the token price back to $1. The question is: what backs up that promise?

Traders hunt price gaps constantly. As long as redemption is guaranteed, any deviation from $1 triggers instant arbitrage — and the arbitrage itself closes the gap. The mechanism only fails if the redemption promise breaks.

Three
mechanisms
Each approach answers the same question differently: what can you always redeem one token for? Click a mechanism to explore it.
Most stable
Fiat-backed
Real dollars held in a bank. One token = one dollar in custody. The simplest and most reliable approach.
Decentralized
Crypto-backed
Backed by crypto collateral locked in smart contracts — but over-collateralized to absorb volatility.
High risk
Algorithmic
No collateral. Uses supply-and-demand mechanics and a paired token to maintain the peg algorithmically.

⚠ Death spiral risk

When confidence breaks, the seigniorage token crashes in value. The protocol can no longer incentivize bond purchases. Supply can't contract. The stablecoin loses its peg permanently. This is precisely what destroyed TerraUST and LUNA in May 2022 — $40 billion in value evaporated in under a week.
